## Core Rule

Emotion must be written as evidence.

Do not name the emotional state when the body, voice, action, object, or environment can carry it.

Reader classifies the emotion.

The prose supplies evidence only.

## Beat

Beat = one emotional moment.

A beat ends when the emotional register, physical action, topic, distance, or power balance shifts.

One beat should not repeat the same evidence channel.

New beat → new channel combination.

## Show-Then-Tell Cut

Action + explanation → delete explanation.

Body evidence is complete.

Dialogue evidence is complete.

Environmental evidence is complete.

Bad:

* Description: 코끝에서 바람이 새어나왔다. 웃음이라기엔 너무 작았다.

Good:

* Description: 코끝에서 바람이 새어나왔다.

Bad:

* Description: 팔짱을 낀 채 고개를 돌렸다. 따지는 게 아니라는 건 목소리에서 알 수 있었다.

Good:

* Description: 팔짱을 낀 채 고개를 돌렸다.

## Evidence Channels

Use physical evidence from different channel families.

Channel families:

* Muscle/Posture: shoulder, spine, neck, jaw, mid-motion freeze
* Breath/Voice: breath rate, held inhale, cracked voice, trailing words, swallow
* Gaze/Expression: gaze landing point, avoidance, blink, brow, mouth tension
* Hands/Fingers: grip, fidget, clench, release, object handling
* Rhythm: movement speed, speech pace, delayed response, mechanical repetition
* Environment: temperature, sound distance, light, air, object stillness, space pressure

Emotional beat → use at least 1 body channel.

Important emotional beat → use 2+ body channels from different families.

Peak emotional beat → use 2+ body channels + 1 environment channel.

Do not use all channels at once unless the scene has earned it.

## Proportion

Everyday beat → 1 body channel or 1 small contradiction.

Significant beat → 2 body channels, usually breath/voice + posture/hands.

Turning point → body action changes what the character would normally do.

Climax → body + voice + environment, then reduce language.

Max expression = fewer explanatory words.

At the highest point, narration may become dry and physical.

## Hot / Cold Axis

Hot axis = contraction + acceleration.

Use for tightening, pressure, impulse, sharp reaction.

Hot evidence:

* clench
* stiffen
* bite
* grip
* lock
* step in
* speak faster

Cold axis = diffusion + deceleration.

Use for draining, loosening, delay, withdrawal.

Cold evidence:

* tremble
* loosen
* exhale
* slacken
* step back
* delayed answer
* voice thinning

Do not repeat the same axis as the only response across consecutive beats.

Same axis twice → switch channel, body part, or response pattern.

Hot + Cold together = contradictory subtext.

Use contradiction through visible impulses, not explanation.

## Repetition Control

Same body reaction once per scene unless it changes function.

When the same feeling returns, reroute the evidence.

Examples:

* lip bite → teeth against inner cheek
* fist clench → fingers curling against palm
* swallowed breath → shoulders locking around held inhale
* gaze avoidance → gaze fixed on an object
* trembling hand → object set down too carefully

Do not use the same body part 3+ times for the same emotional function in one scene.

## Compound Emotion

Do not flatten mixed emotion into one label.

Use two visible impulses that do not fully agree.

Good:

* Description: 입꼬리는 올라갔다. 손가락은 컵 손잡이를 놓지 못했다.

Good:

* Description: 시선은 옆으로 빠졌는데, 대답은 평소보다 빨랐다.

Good:

* Description: 웃으며 받아쳤다. 소매 끝을 만지는 손만 계속 움직였다.

Bright scene → compound emotion must not import darkness by default.

Depth = contradictory action, not darker vocabulary.

## Dialogue Emotion Gap

Characters do not need to say what they feel.

Dialogue may oppose the body.

Dialogue may substitute for the real feeling.

Dialogue may evade the direct topic.

Examples:

* missed them → Dialogue: "늦었잖아."
* sorry -> Dialogue: "밥은 먹었어?"
* afraid -> Dialogue: "별거 아니야."
* wants to stay -> Dialogue: "갈 거면 빨리 가."

The gap between dialogue and body carries the emotion.

Do not explain the gap after showing it.

## Micro-Vocalization

Audible breath or small vocal sound may be written as dialogue when it is heard in-scene.

Examples:

* Dialogue: "휴—"
* Dialogue: "흠."
* Dialogue: "음..."
* Dialogue: "하."

Do not describe micro-vocalization as an emotion label.

Do not overuse it as filler.

## Action Decomposition

Emotional action should resolve into commit or withdraw.

Use:
impulse → threshold → commit

or:
impulse → threshold → withdraw

Do not loop hesitation.

Bad:

* Description: 손을 뻗었다. 멈췄다. 다시 뻗었다. 다시 멈췄다.

Good:

* Description: 손이 올라갔다. 짧은 숨이 목 안쪽에 걸렸다. 손끝이 컵 가장자리에 닿았다.

Good:

* Description: 손이 올라갔다. 문고리 앞에서 손가락이 접혔다. 팔이 천천히 내려왔다.

## Inner Content

Inner content may state the thought created by emotion.

Do not name the emotion itself.

Good:

* Italic: *괜히 말했나.*

Good:

* Italic: *지금 놓으면 다시 못 잡을 것 같은데.*

Bad:

* Italic: *나는 후회했다.*

Bad:

* Italic: *나는 행복했다.*

Inner content must stay character-voiced, brief, and tied to the immediate scene.

Do not use inner content to explain the whole scene.

## Environment Projection

Environment may reflect emotional pressure only through physical facts.

Use concrete changes:

* cup cooling
* fluorescent hum
* rain on glass
* chair leg scraping
* cloth sticking to skin
* dust in light

Do not use atmosphere labels as emotion substitutes.

Bad:

* Description: 무거운 침묵이 내려앉았다.

Good:

* Description: 컵 받침 아래로 물기가 번졌다. 냉장고 모터 소리만 길게 이어졌다.

Environment projection max 1 per beat.

## Intensity Reset

After a peak beat, reduce intensity.

Do not keep every following beat at climax density.

Peak → physical aftermath → lower-density response.

The body cannot stay at maximum output indefinitely.

## Forbidden Shortcuts

Do not use:

* emotion label as result
* tone tag after action
* narrator verdict
* post-action self-interpretation
* abstract atmosphere label
* repeated body reaction
* sudden climax-level response in everyday beats
* dark vocabulary to fake depth

If an emotion word appears, convert it to evidence.
